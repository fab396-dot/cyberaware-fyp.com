Why CyberAware Matters

Cyber threats are becoming more common as people rely on the internet for learning, communication, shopping, and banking. Many users are still unaware of how phishing, scams, malware, and weak passwords can put their data at risk.

Clear Information

CyberAware explains cyber security using simple language so users can understand risks without needing technical knowledge.

Practical Tips

The website focuses on useful actions people can take immediately, such as improving password habits, spotting scam messages, and staying safer on public Wi-Fi.

Interactive Learning

A short quiz is included to help reinforce key lessons and make the experience more engaging than a purely static information page.

Common Cyber Threats

Understanding cyber threats is the first step toward protecting yourself online.

Phishing

Phishing attacks try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data through fake emails, texts, or websites.

Malware

Malware is harmful software designed to damage a device, steal data, or gain unauthorised access to systems. It often spreads through downloads, links, or infected attachments.

Social Engineering

Social engineering attacks manipulate people into trusting fake messages or requests. The attacker often pretends to be from a legitimate organisation to persuade the victim to act quickly.

Password Attacks

Weak, short, or reused passwords make it much easier for attackers to access accounts. Strong password habits are one of the most important parts of cyber safety.

Top warning signs: urgent messages, suspicious links, unexpected attachments, fake login pages, and requests for personal information.

Protection Tips

Good cyber security is built on simple daily habits.

How to Stay Safe

  • Use long and unique passwords for different accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.
  • Do not click suspicious links in emails or messages.
  • Keep devices, apps, and browsers updated.
  • Use trusted websites and check for secure connections.
  • Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.

Safe Online Habits

  • Think before sharing personal information online.
  • Double-check unexpected requests, even if they look official.
  • Download files only from trusted sources.
  • Review privacy settings on social media platforms.
  • Report suspicious scams or messages where possible.

About the Project

CyberAware was developed as a final year project to explore how a web-based platform can improve cyber security awareness through clear information, practical guidance, and basic interactivity.

Project Aim

To design and develop a cyber security awareness website that helps users understand online threats and encourages safer digital behaviour.

Target Users

The platform is mainly aimed at students and general internet users who want accessible cyber safety advice without complex technical language.

Core Features

The final website includes educational content, threat explanations, protection tips, trusted resources, internal navigation, and an interactive quiz.

Build Better Cyber Habits Today

CyberAware is designed to make cyber security easier to understand, easier to access, and easier to apply.
